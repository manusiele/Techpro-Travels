Parks & Reserves
  • South Luangwa National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    South Luangwa National Park is located in eastern Zambia, in the Luangwa Valley. It covers approximately 9,050 square kilometers (3,500 square miles) and is bounded by the Luangwa River to the west. The closest town, Mfuwe, provides access to the park and has an airport connecting it to other parts of Zambia.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    South Luangwa is known for its high density of wildlife, especially around the Luangwa River. Visitors can spot elephants, lions, leopards, giraffes, hippos, and crocodiles, along with large herds of buffalo. The park is also famous for its endemic Thornicroft's giraffe and is an excellent destination for birdwatching, hosting over 400 bird species.

    Safari Lodges

    Safari lodges provide you with a luxurious yet close-to-nature experience in the heart of Africa's game parks. Enjoy comfortable rooms with stunning views, gourmet dining, and easy access to thrilling wildlife safaris—all designed to immerse you in the beauty and excitement of the wild, while offering all the comforts you need to relax and recharge.

    Treehouse Lodges

    Treehouse lodges offer a unique, elevated experience, allowing you to stay high above ground with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and wildlife. Designed to blend seamlessly with nature, these lodges provide comfort and adventure, giving you a one-of-a-kind opportunity to immerse yourself in the beauty of the wilderness from a bird’s-eye perspective.

  • Lower Zambezi National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Lower Zambezi National Park is located along the northwestern banks of the Zambezi River in southeastern Zambia, directly across from Zimbabwe’s Mana Pools National Park. It spans approximately 4,092 square kilometers (1,580 square miles) and offers scenic riverfront landscapes.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Overview

    The park is rich in wildlife, including elephants, hippos, lions, leopards, and various antelope species. The Zambezi River attracts a large population of waterbirds and provides excellent fishing opportunities. Wildlife viewing is especially rewarding along the riverbanks, with unique opportunities to observe animals from boats and canoes.

    Luxury Villas

    Luxury villas in game parks provide a private, high-end retreat amidst the wilderness, combining elegance with adventure. With exclusive amenities, breathtaking views, and spacious accommodations, these villas allow you to experience nature in comfort and style, offering the ultimate blend of luxury and proximity to wildlife.

    Rondavels

    Rondavels are traditional, round, thatched-roof huts that provide a cozy, authentic stay in the heart of nature. Often located in scenic areas within game parks, these charming accommodations offer a simple yet comfortable space, blending cultural heritage with a close connection to the surrounding environment.

  • Kafue National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Kafue National Park is the largest park in Zambia, covering about 22,400 square kilometers (8,650 square miles) in the central part of the country. The park is located west of the Kafue River and is accessible from the town of Mongu to the west and Lusaka to the east.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Kafue hosts a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, lions, leopards, cheetahs, hippos, and African wild dogs. It is known for its diversity of antelope species, including roan, sable, and puku. The park also has significant birdlife, with over 500 species recorded, making it a paradise for birdwatchers.

    Luxury Villas

    Experience Amboseli in a luxury safari house with views of Mount Kilimanjaro, elegant suites, a private pool, and gourmet dining. Wake up to wildlife like elephants and lions nearby, and unwind with a sunset drink overlooking the savannah. This retreat offers both adventure and relaxation in an unforgettable setting.

    Rondavels

    This luxurious rondavel blends traditional African charm with modern comfort. Featuring a thatched roof, hand-carved accents, and open-plan interiors, it offers cozy seating, plush bedding, and panoramic views. Relax on the private deck, enjoying starlit skies and the sounds of nature—an elegant, authentic African retreat.

    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Luambe National Park is a smaller, remote park located in eastern Zambia between South and North Luangwa National Parks, along the eastern bank of the Luangwa River. It covers an area of about 254 square kilometers (98 square miles).

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Ecosystem

    Despite its small size, Luambe National Park offers a variety of wildlife, including elephants, hippos, crocodiles, and various antelope species. The park is especially known for its high concentration of hippos in the Luangwa River and provides excellent birdwatching opportunities.

  • North Luangwa National Park
    • DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    North Luangwa National Park is located in northern Zambia, bordering the Luangwa River. Covering approximately 4,636 square kilometers (1,790 square miles), it is known for its pristine wilderness and limited access, with controlled entry for guided tours and walking safaris.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    North Luangwa National Park is renowned for its remoteness and offers sightings of large animals such as elephants, lions, buffalo, and the endangered black rhino. The park is also home to hippos, leopards, and numerous antelope species, and it is well-regarded for walking safaris, providing an intimate wilderness experience.
