    Location

    Masai Mara National Reserve is located in south west Kenya and is a vast scenic expanse of gently rolling African savannah plains measuring 1510 square kilometers in area and bordering the Serengeti National Park in Tanzania to the south. Masai Mara is a unique wildlife conservation haven famous for its spectacular natural diversity of wildlife and is the premier Kenya Safari location in East Africa, offering visitors numerous reasons to visit this animal paradise. Large numbers of Lions, Cheetah, Elephant, Rhino, African Buffalo, Wildebeest, Giraffe, Zebra and many more animals are found in the park in their natural habitat, unconfined and free to roam the vast Kenyan wilderness stretching for miles on end.

  • Nairobi National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Nairobi National Park is located just 7 kilometers (about 4 miles) south of Nairobi's city center, making it the closest national park to a capital city in the world. The park covers an area of approximately 117 square kilometers (45 square miles) and is bordered by the Nairobi National Park East Road and the Nairobi River to the east. It features a variety of ecosystems, including savannah, wetlands, and grasslands, which support a diverse range of wildlife.

    Ecosystem

    Nairobi National Park is home to a surprising variety of wildlife despite its proximity to the city. The park is famous for its populations of black and white rhinoceroses, which are part of conservation efforts to protect these endangered species. Visitors can also encounter lions, giraffes, zebras, buffaloes, and various antelope species, such as Thomson's gazelle and impala. The park's diverse ecosystems support over 100 species of mammals and more than 400 species of birds, making it a haven for birdwatchers as well. Notable bird species include ostriches, eagles, and various waterfowl that can be seen around the park's wetlands. The unique combination of wildlife and urban backdrop makes Nairobi National Park a remarkable destination for both tourists and locals.

    Location

    Tsavo East National Park is located in southeastern Kenya, approximately 150 kilometers (93 miles) from Nairobi. It covers an area of about 11,747 square kilometers (4,536 square miles), making it one of the largest national parks in Kenya. The park is situated near the town of Voi and is bordered by the Tsavo West National Park to the west. It features diverse landscapes, including open savannahs, thorny bushlands, and riverine forests along the Tsavo River

    Wildlife

    Tsavo East National Park boasts an incredible diversity of wildlife, making it one of Kenya's premier safari destinations. The park is particularly famous for its large herds of elephants, which thrive in the vast open savannahs. Lions can often be seen lounging in the shade or hunting in the tall grass, while leopards, though more elusive, inhabit the wooded areas. Buffaloes graze in herds, and various antelope species, including Grant's gazelles and impalas, are abundant throughout the park. Additionally, Tsavo East is home to an array of unique wildlife, such as Rothschild's giraffes and Burchell's zebras, creating a vibrant ecosystem. Bird enthusiasts will also find over 500 species of birds, ranging from raptors to colorful ground birds, making the park a haven for avifauna. This rich biodiversity ensures that visitors to Tsavo East National Park experience the thrill of encountering a wide variety of animals in their natural habitats.
