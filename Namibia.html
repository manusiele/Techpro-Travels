Parks & Reserves
  • Etosha National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Etosha National Park is located in northern Namibia, covering an area of approximately 22,270 square kilometers (8,600 square miles). The park is centered around the vast Etosha Pan, a large salt flat that forms a significant feature of the landscape. The nearest town is Tsumeb, about 110 kilometers (68 miles) to the south. The park is accessible via the Andersson Gate in the south.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Etosha National Park is renowned for its diverse wildlife, including large populations of elephants, lions, leopards, black and white rhinos, and various antelope species such as springbok, kudu, and oryx. The park also hosts over 340 bird species, making it a prime destination for birdwatching.

    Onguma The Fort:

    Onguma The Fort is a luxurious lodge located just outside Etosha National Park, offering stunning views of the surrounding bushveld and the nearby Etosha Pan. The lodge is designed to resemble an African fortress, with stone walls, sweeping arches, and an impressive structure. Each of the 10 luxury rooms is elegantly decorated, with private balconies overlooking the waterhole where wildlife such as elephants, lions, and rhinos frequently visit.

    Etosha Village:

    Etosha Village offers comfortable, family-friendly accommodation near the park’s Andersson Gate. The lodge features well-appointed thatched chalets and tents, each with en-suite bathrooms and private verandas. The lodge is designed to blend with the surrounding nature and provides a relaxed atmosphere. Guests can enjoy game drives into Etosha, a swimming pool, a bar, and a restaurant serving delicious local and international dishes.

  • Namib-Naukluft National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Namib-Naukluft National Park is situated in southwestern Namibia and covers an area of about 49,768 square kilometers (19,132 square miles). It encompasses the Namib Desert, one of the oldest deserts in the world, and is bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the west. The park is accessible via several gates, with the main entrance being near Sesriem.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Diversity

    The Namib-Naukluft National Park is known for its unique desert wildlife, including oryx, springbok, and desert-adapted elephants. The park is also home to a variety of reptiles and insects. While wildlife density may be lower compared to other parks, the stunning landscapes and geological formations, such as the iconic Sossusvlei dunes, draw visitors from around the world.

    Onguma The Fort

    Onguma The Fort is a luxurious lodge located just outside Etosha National Park, offering stunning views of the surrounding bushveld and the nearby Etosha Pan. The lodge is designed to resemble an African fortress, with stone walls, sweeping arches, and an impressive structure. Each of the 10 luxury rooms is elegantly decorated, with private balconies overlooking the waterhole where wildlife such as elephants, lions, and rhinos frequently visit. Onguma The Fort provides guests with exceptional service, a restaurant offering gourmet meals, and the chance to experience game drives within Etosha.

    Halali Camp:

    Halali Camp is located in the heart of Etosha National Park, making it one of the most convenient options for exploring the park's wildlife. The camp offers simple but comfortable accommodation, including chalets, family rooms, and camping sites. The camp is situated near the popular Moringa Waterhole, where guests can view wildlife right from the comfort of the camp’s observation deck. Halali Camp features a swimming pool, a restaurant, and a bar, providing all the basic amenities for a relaxing stay.

  • Skeleton Coast National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Skeleton Coast National Park is located in the northwestern part of Namibia, along the Atlantic Ocean. It stretches approximately 500 kilometers (310 miles) from the Ugab River in the south to the Angola border in the north. The park features rugged coastline, sand dunes, and desolate landscapes.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Overview

    Skeleton Coast National Park is known for its unique desert-adapted wildlife, including elephants, lions, and the rare brown hyena. The park's marine life is also remarkable, with seals, dolphins, and various seabird species along the coast. The stark landscapes and shipwrecks add to the park's eerie beauty.

    Sossusvlei Desert Lodge:

    Sossusvlei Desert Lodge offers an exclusive and luxurious experience in the heart of the Namib Desert, with stunning views of the surrounding dunes and rugged landscapes. The lodge features spacious, elegant suites with floor-to-ceiling windows, private plunge pools, and expansive terraces, allowing guests to fully immerse themselves in the desert's beauty. Guests can enjoy a range of activities, including game drives, dune excursions, stargazing, and guided walks. The lodge’s restaurant serves exquisite meals, often featuring locally sourced ingredients. Sossusvlei Desert Lodge is perfect for those looking for a combination of comfort, adventure, and a connection to the desert landscape.

    Kulala Desert Lodge:

    Kulala Desert Lodge, situated near the famous Sossusvlei dunes, offers an intimate and rustic desert experience. The lodge features well-appointed thatched chalets with private verandas, providing stunning views of the surrounding desert and dunes. Guests can enjoy various activities such as nature walks, hot air balloon rides, and game drives. Kulala Desert Lodge is known for its excellent service and its close proximity to the Sossusvlei dunes, making it an ideal base for exploring the park's iconic landmarks.

  • Bwabwata National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Bwabwata National Park is situated in northeastern Namibia, near the Caprivi Strip. It covers an area of about 6,274 square kilometers (2,426 square miles) and is bordered by the Zambezi River to the north and the Kwando River to the south. The park is part of the Kavango-Zambezi Transfrontier Conservation Area.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Bwabwata National Park is known for its diverse wildlife, including large populations of elephants and buffaloes. The park also hosts species such as lions, leopards, and numerous antelope species, including the endangered roan and sable antelope. Its rich birdlife includes various waterfowl and raptors, making it a fantastic destination for birdwatching.

    Shipwreck Lodge:

    Shipwreck Lodge is an exclusive and luxurious lodge situated in the heart of the Skeleton Coast National Park, offering guests a unique and remote experience. The lodge is inspired by the area’s rich history of shipwrecks, with individual villas designed to resemble the remains of ships washed ashore. Each villa offers stunning views of the desert and coastline, with spacious interiors, private decks, and en-suite bathrooms. The lodge offers a variety of activities, including guided game drives, nature walks, and visits to the nearby shipwrecks and seal colonies.

    Mowe Bay Camp:

    Mowe Bay Camp is a small, intimate lodge located on the edge of the Skeleton Coast, offering a more rustic and immersive desert experience. The camp is set in a remote area of the park, providing guests with an opportunity to connect with the natural environment. Mowe Bay offers comfortable tented accommodation, each with en-suite bathrooms and a private veranda overlooking the desert. Guests can enjoy a range of activities, including guided desert excursions, birdwatching, and visits to the nearby shipwrecks and seal colonies.

  • Waterberg Plateau Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Waterberg Plateau Park is located in central Namibia, near the town of Otjiwarongo. The park covers an area of about 405 square kilometers (156 square miles) and is known for its dramatic sandstone plateau rising steeply from the surrounding plains.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Waterberg Plateau Park is home to a variety of wildlife, including endangered species like the black rhino and the African wild dog. Other species include giraffes, zebras, and various antelope species. The park's diverse habitats range from savannahs to lush vegetation, providing a home for numerous bird species, making it an excellent spot for birdwatching.

    Mbamba Camp: Located in the heart of Bwabwata National Park, Mbamba Camp offers an intimate and authentic experience for nature lovers. The camp features comfortable, spacious tents with en-suite bathrooms and private decks overlooking the surrounding wilderness. Guests can enjoy activities like boat trips along the Zambezi River, game drives, and guided walking safaris, all led by knowledgeable local guides. Mbamba Camp is known for its relaxed atmosphere, and its proximity to both the park’s wildlife and the river makes it an ideal base for exploring the park’s diverse ecosystems.
    Ntwetwe Lodge:

    Ntwetwe Lodge is a small, eco-friendly lodge located on the edge of Bwabwata National Park, offering a tranquil escape in the African wilderness. The lodge features comfortable chalets with en-suite bathrooms and private verandas that provide views of the surrounding bush. Ntwetwe Lodge specializes in eco-tourism, and guests can engage in activities like bird watching, game viewing, and cultural visits to local communities. The lodge is also known for its warm hospitality and delicious meals, often made with locally sourced ingredients.
