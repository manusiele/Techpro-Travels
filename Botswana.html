Parks & Reserves
  • Okavango Delta
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    The Okavango Delta is a unique inland delta located in northern Botswana, formed by the Okavango River. It is renowned for its stunning wetlands, rich biodiversity, and complex ecosystems, providing a habitat for a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, hippos, and numerous bird species. The delta is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is famous for its annual flood that transforms the landscape, making it a premier destination for safari and eco-tourism.

    Diversity

    The Okavango Delta is home to a rich diversity of wildlife, including large populations of elephants, hippos, crocodiles, and various antelope species like lechwe and reedbuck. The delta also hosts numerous bird species, including the majestic African fish eagle and vibrant kingfishers. Predators like lions, leopards, and wild dogs thrive in this unique ecosystem.

    Luxury Safari Lodges and Camps

    The Okavango Delta is home to some of Africa's most exclusive and luxurious safari lodges and tented camps. These lodges often feature spacious, well-appointed tents or suites with private decks, plunge pools, and spectacular views. Many are located on private concessions to provide a more exclusive experience. Notable luxury properties include Sanctuary Baines Camp, Vumbura Plains Camp, and Chiefâ€™s Camp, which offer exceptional service, gourmet dining, and guided safaris.

    Mobile Safari Camps

    For a more adventurous experience, mobile camps set up temporary sites in the delta, offering a combination of luxury and a sense of wilderness. These camps are typically more intimate and eco-friendly, with tented accommodations and communal dining areas. Activities may include game drives, boat safaris, and walking safaris.

  • Moremi Game Reserve
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Moremi Game Reserve is situated in the eastern side of the Okavango Delta and is recognized for its incredible wildlife diversity. The reserve encompasses various habitats, including wetlands, grasslands, and forested areas, making it home to a rich array of species, including elephants, giraffes, and many bird species. Moremi is renowned for its exceptional game viewing and offers visitors a chance to experience the beauty of the delta and its wildlife.

    Visit Official Website
    Interactive Map
    Overview

    Moremi Game Reserve is home to an incredible variety of wildlife, including elephants, giraffes, and buffaloes. The reserve also hosts predators such as lions, leopards, and hyenas. Birdwatchers can enjoy spotting over 400 species of birds, including the colorful lilac-breasted roller and various waterfowl.

    Xakanaxa Camp

    Located on the edge of the Khwai River in Moremi Game Reserve, Xakanaxa Camp offers a luxurious tented safari experience. It features spacious tents with en-suite bathrooms, a restaurant, and a bar overlooking the river. Activities include game drives and boat safaris, with excellent opportunities to observe wildlife along the river. This high-end camp provides an intimate and immersive experience with stunning wildlife and views.

    Savute Safari Lodge

    Located on the Savute Channel in the western part of Moremi Game Reserve, Savute Safari Lodge is known for its prime wildlife sightings, especially elephants and lions. The lodge offers luxury tented accommodation with views of the channel, a pool, a restaurant, and bar. Guests can enjoy game drives and boat trips along the channel, making it perfect for those seeking an exclusive and remote safari experience. Price Range

  • Makgadikgadi Pan National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Makgadikgadi Pan National Park is located in northeastern Botswana and is characterized by vast salt pans and semi-arid landscapes. Once part of a large lake, the pans are now home to unique wildlife adapted to the harsh environment, including desert-adapted elephants and various bird species. The park offers a range of activities, including quad biking, wildlife viewing, and cultural experiences with the local San people.

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Overview

    In Makgadikgadi Pan National Park, visitors can see unique desert-adapted wildlife, including the rare brown hyena and large herds of springbok. During the rainy season, the pans attract flocks of flamingos and various migratory birds. The park also hosts elephants and other wildlife that come to the waterholes.

    San Camp

    Situated in the heart of the Makgadikgadi Pans, San Camp offers a luxurious, remote experience amidst the salt pans. Guests can enjoy guided walks with the indigenous San people, quad biking across the pans, and wildlife viewing, including meerkats and migratory birds. The camp offers spacious tents with en-suite bathrooms, and the atmosphere is serene and exclusive.

    Meno a Kwena

    Overlooking the Boteti River, Meno a Kwena provides a more intimate experience with luxury tents offering spectacular views. The camp offers game drives, cultural experiences, and visits to nearby salt pans, providing great opportunities to view large herds of elephants, zebras, and other wildlife along the river.
