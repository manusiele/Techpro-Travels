Parks & Reserves
  • Kruger National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Kruger National Park is located in northeastern South Africa, spanning across the provinces of Limpopo and Mpumalanga. It covers approximately 19,485 square kilometers (7,523 square miles) and is one of Africa's largest game reserves

    Visit Official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Kruger National Park is home to the "Big Five" (lions, leopards, elephants, buffaloes, and rhinoceroses) as well as a vast array of wildlife, including cheetahs, hippos, crocodiles, and over 500 bird species. The park supports a diverse ecosystem with various habitats ranging from savannahs to woodlands.

    Safari Lodges

    Safari lodges provide you with a luxurious yet close-to-nature experience in the heart of Africa's game parks. Enjoy comfortable rooms with stunning views, gourmet dining, and easy access to thrilling wildlife safaris—all designed to immerse you in the beauty and excitement of the wild, while offering all the comforts you need to relax and recharge.

    Treehouse Lodges

    Treehouse lodges offer a unique, elevated experience, allowing you to stay high above ground with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and wildlife. Designed to blend seamlessly with nature, these lodges provide comfort and adventure, giving you a one-of-a-kind opportunity to immerse yourself in the beauty of the wilderness from a bird’s-eye perspective.

  • Addo Elephant National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Located in southern Kenya at the foot of Mount Kilimanjaro, is renowned for its stunning landscapes and diverse wildlife, particularly its large herds of African elephants. The park features lush swamps and marshes that attract a variety of animals, including lions, giraffes, and numerous bird species, making it a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy guided game drives, walking safaris, and cultural experiences with the local Maasai community, who coexist harmoniously with the wildlife. Best visited during the dry seasons from June to October, Amboseli offers breathtaking views, especially of Kilimanjaro, and a unique opportunity to engage with nature and culture in a remarkable setting.

    Visit Official Website
    Interactive Map
    Ecosystem

    Addo Elephant National Park is famous for its large elephant population and is also home to lions, buffaloes, black rhinos, and a variety of antelope species. The park is unique for its diverse habitats, including coastal dunes, and supports a range of birdlife, including the endangered African black oystercatcher.

    Luxury Villas

    Luxury villas in game parks provide a private, high-end retreat amidst the wilderness, combining elegance with adventure. With exclusive amenities, breathtaking views, and spacious accommodations, these villas allow you to experience nature in comfort and style, offering the ultimate blend of luxury and proximity to wildlife.

    Rondavels

    Rondavels are traditional, round, thatched-roof huts that provide a cozy, authentic stay in the heart of nature. Often located in scenic areas within game parks, these charming accommodations offer a simple yet comfortable space, blending cultural heritage with a close connection to the surrounding environment.

  • Pilanesberg National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Pilanesberg National Park is located in the North West Province of South Africa, approximately 150 kilometers (93 miles) northwest of Johannesburg. The park covers an area of about 572 square kilometers (221 square miles).

    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Pilanesberg National Park is home to the "Big Five" and features a diverse array of wildlife, including elephants, lions, leopards, buffaloes, and rhinoceroses. The park's varied habitats also support species such as giraffes, zebras, and numerous bird species, making it a great destination for wildlife viewing.

    Luxury Villas

    Experience Amboseli in a luxury safari house with views of Mount Kilimanjaro, elegant suites, a private pool, and gourmet dining. Wake up to wildlife like elephants and lions nearby, and unwind with a sunset drink overlooking the savannah. This retreat offers both adventure and relaxation in an unforgettable setting.

    Rondavels

    This luxurious rondavel blends traditional African charm with modern comfort. Featuring a thatched roof, hand-carved accents, and open-plan interiors, it offers cozy seating, plush bedding, and panoramic views. Relax on the private deck, enjoying starlit skies and the sounds of nature—an elegant, authentic African retreat.

  • Hluhluwe-iMfolozi Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Hluhluwe-iMfolozi Park is located in KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, approximately 280 kilometers (174 miles) north of Durban. The park covers around 96,000 hectares (236,000 acres).

    Interactive Map
    Ecosystem

    Hluhluwe-iMfolozi Park is known for its conservation efforts for the white rhinoceros and is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, lions, leopards, buffaloes, and various antelope species. The park also boasts a rich avifauna with over 400 bird species recorded.

    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Sabi Sand Game Reserve is located adjacent to Kruger National Park in Mpumalanga, South Africa. The reserve covers an area of approximately 65,000 hectares (160,000 acres).

    Visit official Website
    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Sabi Sand Game Reserve is famous for its high density of wildlife, including the "Big Five." The reserve is particularly renowned for its leopard sightings. Other wildlife includes elephants, lions, cheetahs, and a variety of antelope species. The reserve is also known for its luxury lodges and exclusive safari experiences.
