Game Parks & Reserves
  • Serengeti National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Serengeti National Park is located in northern Tanzania, East Africa. It spans approximately 14,750 square kilometers (5,700 square miles) and lies close to the Kenyan border, connecting with the Maasai Mara Reserve. The park is situated west of the Great Rift Valley and extends to Lake Victoria's southeastern shore. The nearest town is Arusha, about 335 kilometers (208 miles) to the southeast, which serves as a primary gateway for visitors traveling to the Serengeti.

    Visit Official Website
    Interactive Map
    Diversity

    Serengeti National Park is home to an incredible diversity of wildlife. It boasts one of the largest populations of mammals in Africa, including the "Big Five": lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, and rhinoceroses. The park is also famous for its vast herds of wildebeest and zebras that migrate annually, alongside various antelope species like Thomson's and Grant's gazelles. Predators such as cheetahs, hyenas, and jackals thrive here, while the avifauna includes over 500 bird species, ranging from raptors to vibrant ground birds. The rich ecosystem supports a complex web of life, making the Serengeti a premier destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

  • Ngorongoro Crater
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Ngorongoro Crater, located in Tanzania's Ngorongoro Conservation Area, is one of the world's largest intact volcanic calderas, spanning about 260 square kilometers (100 square miles). Renowned for its stunning scenery and rich biodiversity, the crater is home to diverse wildlife, including the "Big Five." It sits at an elevation of approximately 2,286 meters (7,474 feet) above sea level and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The nearest town, Karatu, is about 20 kilometers (12 miles) away, providing access for visitors exploring the area.

    Visit Official Website
    Interactive Map
    Diversity

    Ngorongoro Crater is home to a rich diversity of wildlife due to its unique ecosystem. The crater supports a variety of species, including the "Big Five": lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, and rhinoceroses. The area is also inhabited by large populations of wildebeest, zebras, and various antelope species such as Thomson's and Grant's gazelles. Additionally, the crater is a sanctuary for many birds, with over 500 species recorded, including flamingos and raptors. Its closed environment and abundant water sources make it a premier destination for observing wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Tarangire National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Tarangire National Park is located in northern Tanzania, approximately 120 kilometers (75 miles) southwest of Arusha. The park covers an area of about 2,850 square kilometers (1,100 square miles) and is known for its diverse ecosystems, including savannahs, woodlands, and wetlands. Tarangire is situated along the Tarangire River, which attracts a large variety of wildlife, particularly during the dry season. The park is easily accessible from other popular tourist destinations, such as Serengeti National Park and Ngorongoro Crater, making it a key part of Tanzania's northern safari circuit.

    Visit Official Website
    Interactive Map
    Ecosystem

    Tarangire National Park is renowned for its impressive diversity of wildlife, featuring large populations of elephants, often seen in herds around the Tarangire River. The park is also home to predators like lions, leopards, and cheetahs, alongside various antelope species, including impalas, Grant's gazelles, and the unique fringe-eared oryx. Additionally, Tarangire hosts a rich birdlife, with over 500 species recorded, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. The park's mix of habitats, including woodlands and swamps, supports this vibrant ecosystem, offering visitors excellent opportunities for wildlife viewing year-round.

  • Lake Manyara National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Lake Manyara National Park is located in northern Tanzania, approximately 126 kilometers (78 miles) west of Arusha. The park covers an area of about 330 square kilometers (127 square miles) and is situated along the eastern edge of the Great Rift Valley, with its western boundary bordering Lake Manyara. The park is easily accessible from the main road between Arusha and the Serengeti, making it a popular stop for travelers exploring northern Tanzania. Known for its diverse ecosystems, the park features a mix of ground water forest, woodlands, and the alkaline lake, providing a habitat for a variety of wildlife, including elephants, giraffes, and numerous bird species.

    Interactive Map
    Wildlife

    Lake Manyara National Park is known for its rich biodiversity and diverse wildlife. The park is famous for its large herds of elephants, which can often be seen roaming the forests and grasslands. Visitors may also encounter giraffes, zebras, buffaloes, and a variety of antelope species, including impalas and waterbucks. The park's unique ecosystem supports a wide range of bird species, with over 400 recorded, including flamingos, pelicans, and the striking African fish eagle. Additionally, Lake Manyara is home to a variety of primates, such as blue monkeys and baboons. This vibrant wildlife, set against the stunning backdrop of the lake and surrounding landscapes, makes Lake Manyara a remarkable destination for nature enthusiasts and safari-goers.

  • Ruaha National Park
    •
    DESCRIPTION ACCOMODATION
    Location

    Ruaha National Park is located in central Tanzania, approximately 130 kilometers (81 miles) west of Iringa. Covering an area of about 20,226 square kilometers (7,809 square miles), it is the largest national park in Tanzania. The park is situated in the Ruaha River Valley, which is a key feature of the park's landscape. Ruaha is accessible from Iringa via a gravel road, making it a remote yet captivating destination for wildlife enthusiasts. The park's diverse ecosystems range from savannah and woodland to riverine habitats, offering a home to a rich variety of flora and fauna.

    Interactive Map
    Diversity

    Ruaha National Park is renowned for its abundant and diverse wildlife, making it a prime destination for safari enthusiasts. The park is home to a large population of elephants, with herds frequently seen along the Ruaha River. Visitors can also encounter predators such as lions, leopards, and cheetahs, as well as packs of African wild dogs. Additionally, the park boasts a variety of herbivores, including giraffes, buffaloes, kudu, and impalas. Ruaha is also known for its impressive birdlife, with over 570 species recorded, including raptors and migratory birds. This rich ecosystem, characterized by its varied landscapes, offers excellent opportunities for wildlife viewing throughout the year.
